In the near future...

driver-less cars will dominate the highways. Much like the popularity of cloud services where customers no longer own software, people will no longer own cars. They will simply order a ride on demand. The expense would be offset by the lack of car payment, auto insurance, vehicle maintenance and fuel costs. Car dealerships and rental car companies will be converted to driver-less car parking and service lots. Private companies will also appear offering their own spin on the driver-less car experience. The need for driver-less car management apps is upon us.

AutoMate is...

a driver-less car app that allows users to request and schedule rides. A user will sign up, log in, enter a location, destination and time. Vehicle options will include economy, mid-size, luxury and carpool. Users can order immediate one-way or round-trip rides and schedule future pick-ups or recurring rides for daily commute. Google Maps will calculate distance and commute times for pricing. Users will be shown time until pickup, ride time and cost before confirming their order with a button click at which time, their order will be entered into a database and a vehicle engaged for pick-up.

Once the vehicle arrives...

a txt message is sent to the customer's phone prompting them that the vehicle has arrived and to confirm by responding to the txt with the confirmation number once they are safely in the vehicle and their seatbelt is fastened. The ride is complete once the vehicle has reached its destination address. The driver-less car will return to the service lot for refueling and maintenance.
